# 🧪 chemistry-sds-ai-review

## 📖 Overview

This case study evaluates AI-generated Safety Data Sheet (SDS) content against **GHS** (*Globally Harmonized System of Classification and Labelling of Chemicals*) and **OSHA Hazard Communication Standard (HazCom 2012)** requirements, comparing AI outputs against established chemistry and regulatory best practices.

The objective is to identify inaccuracies, assess potential safety impacts, and demonstrate the importance of domain-expert review within AI-assisted chemistry workflows.

---

## 🔬 Methodology

Each case study includes:

1. **AI-Generated SDS Content** – Original output produced by an AI system.
2. **Expert Error Analysis** – Identification of technical, regulatory, or safety-related issues.
3. **Error Classification** – Categorization of findings based on risk and compliance impact.
4. **Corrected SDS Version** – Revised content aligned with regulatory and scientific standards.
5. **Annotation Rationale** – Explanation supporting each correction.
6. **Severity Scoring** – Risk assessment using a **1–5 scale**:

| Severity | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | Minor wording or formatting issue |
| 2 | Low-risk technical inaccuracy |
| 3 | Moderate compliance concern |
| 4 | Significant safety or regulatory error |
| 5 | Critical error with potential harm or serious compliance implications |

---

## ⚠️ Error Categories Identified

### GHS Hazard Misclassification
Examples include:

- Incorrect hazard class assignments
- Wrong signal words (*Danger* vs. *Warning*)
- Missing or incorrect hazard pictograms
- Inaccurate hazard statements

### Exposure Limit Errors

Examples include:

- Missing OSHA Permissible Exposure Limits (PEL)
- Incorrect ACGIH Threshold Limit Values (TLV)
- Omitted occupational exposure guidance

### Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) Deficiencies

Examples include:

- Incomplete PPE recommendations
- Missing respiratory protection guidance
- Insufficient eye, skin, or hand protection requirements

### First Aid Inaccuracies

Examples include:

- Incorrect emergency response instructions
- Missing exposure-specific first aid measures
- Outdated medical treatment recommendations

### Disposal and Environmental Compliance Issues

Examples include:

- Improper disposal instructions
- Missing environmental hazard considerations
- Non-compliant waste handling guidance

---

## 🤖 Why This Matters for AI

Errors in AI-generated chemistry and safety documentation can create:

- Worker safety risks
- Regulatory compliance violations
- Environmental liabilities
- Operational and legal exposure

This repository demonstrates how expert review and annotation improve the reliability of AI-generated SDS content and highlights the critical role of subject matter expertise in chemistry-focused AI data pipelines.

---

## 🎯 Project Goals

- Evaluate AI performance on chemistry and regulatory content
- Identify common failure patterns in SDS generation
- Develop high-quality annotated correction datasets
- Improve AI safety, compliance, and reliability in scientific domains
- Support future chemistry-focused model training and evaluation efforts

---

## 🧩 Key Takeaway

**AI can accelerate documentation workflows, but chemistry and safety-critical content still require expert validation.** Proper annotation and review help ensure regulatory compliance, scientific accuracy, and protection of human health and the environment.
